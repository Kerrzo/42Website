### **Understanding the impact of VR on the brain**

Virtual reality technology can have a significant impact on the brain. When we experience VR, we are essentially tricking our brain into believing that we are in a different environment. This immersive experience can create a sense of presence, where the individual feels as though they are physically present in the virtual environment. This sense of presence can lead to a reduction in stress and anxiety levels, as the brain perceives the virtual environment as a safe space.

### **Neurological benefits of relaxation**

Relaxation has been shown to have numerous neurological benefits. When we are in a state of relaxation, our brain shifts from the sympathetic nervous system (fight or flight response) to the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest response). This shift leads to a decrease in heart rate, blood pressure, and muscle tension, and an increase in oxygen levels. These physiological changes can lead to a decrease in stress and anxiety levels and an improvement in overall well-being.

### **Clinical research on Virtual Tour Relaxation Therapy**
