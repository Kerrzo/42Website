Technology and economics have always been two major forces shaping how customers interact with businesses. In recent years, however, advances in [technology](https://www.42interactive.com/) have given customers more control than ever before. This shift has led businesses to focus increasingly on customer experience. Delivering a great customer experience is now essential for businesses that want to succeed in the digital age.

‍

## What is Digital Transformation?

‍

Digital transformation is the process of integrating digital technology into all aspects of a business. This results in significant changes to an organisation's operation and the delivery of their value to the customers. It is about changing the way a business interacts with its customers and how they provide them with a better experience whenever and wherever they need it. The customer experience is important in business and digital transformation. Improving customer experience leads to innovation, growth, and success.

‍

Digital transformation goes beyond just digitalising projects and affects the whole organisation. It requires a shift in thinking away from traditional methods and towards a more collaborative, experimental approach. This can reveal new solutions which, in turn, improve customer experience - one of the benefits of taking this digital transformation journey.

## What Is the Connection Between Digital Transformation and Customer Experience?

‍

In today's tech-driven climate, excellent customer experience is the new standard bearer for digital businesses. As such, optimising the end-to-end customer experience has become a top priority for companies across all industries. And rightly so - research has shown that investing in customer experience leads to increased business revenue. So if you're looking to digitally transform your company, improving customer experience should be at the top of your list.

‍

It is essential for any organisation to be focused on creating the best possible version of customer experience from the customer's perspective. The goal is to make customers satisfied with their experience with the product or service, in order to ensure customer loyalty and, as a result, recommendations to others. Therefore, it is necessary to prioritise the customer's needs and expectations.

‍

Customers today expect to find content that's relevant to what they're doing, no matter where they are or what device they're using. And that means that digital transformation strategy can't be about just one thing. To really understand the customer experience, you have to take a holistic view of the customer journey. Only then can you see the full impact of digital transformation on customer experience.

## How Can Digital Transformation Enhance the Customer Experience?

‍

For the organisation, digital [transformation](https://www.42interactive.com/services/digital-design-and-development) can help boost profitability, streamline operations and processes, improve customer experience, increase efficiency, and create opportunities to innovate. Furthermore, it helps organisations becoming more agile and able to respond faster to changes in the market or customer demands. Additionally, it can provide a competitive advantage and a way to differentiate an organisation from its competitors. With the right strategies in place, digital transformation can lead to greater success — both now and in the future.

‍

From the customer's point of view, the digital transformation of an organisation can bring greater transparency, less information asymmetry, and new customer benefits such as new products and services, greater convenience, more choice, new experiences, and lower prices. These potential costs such as cognitive and/or tangible investments, learning, information and activity overload, and risks such as loss of privacy and performance uncertainty are worth it when experiencing the advantages first hand.

‍

## Digital Transformation - Impacts and Benefits in Customer Experience

‍

Digital transformation can have a big impact on every part of a business. But the customer is the one who feels it most. The outcomes of digital transformation must be carefully considered. When an organisation goes through digital transformation, remember to keep the focus on providing value to the customer. The goal is to create a better customer experience, not to simply be considered digitally transformed or to keep up with competitors. It's unwise to invest in new features just for the sake of having them. Instead, focus on creating an app that will improve the customer experience in some way.

‍

If an organisation is able to look for areas to improve the customer experience and use the technology that could help solve those issues, the customer experience will be tremendously improved. After all, if an organisation's digital transformation is truly focused on supporting and engaging customers, it will really help to ultimately achieve that customer experience goal.

‍

And, since business owners and marketers now have access to more data on users' digital journeys, it can be extremely helpful to use that data to find insights about customer behaviour. By processing the data and analysing it to improve the product and customer engagement strategy, it can help in finding ways to integrate new customer experience technologies and introduce more creative ways that will keep customers engaged with your product.
